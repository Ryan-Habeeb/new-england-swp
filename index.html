Our history is everywhere around us, even in your backyard.

New England is home to an estimated 240,000 miles worth of historically significant stone walls. Between the late 1700s and early 1800s, European settlers once cleared New England’s forests for local farmland, building these stone walls as property barriers. Over time, through a rapidly changing America, these farmers eventually abandoned their plots for better soil and opportunities. While the forests of New England took back the land once more, these stone walls still stand, reminding New Englander’s of our history and evolution from such a different era.
